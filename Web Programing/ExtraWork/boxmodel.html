Ladakh, a union territory in India, is renowned for its stunning landscapes, high-altitude mountain passes, and vibrant Buddhist culture. Key attractions include Pangong Tso, Nubra Valley, and Leh Palace. Popular activities include trekking, mountain biking, and monastery visits. The best time to visit is generally between March and July. Tourism is one of the economic contributors to the union territory of Ladakh in Northern India. This union territory is located between the Karakoram mountain range to the north and the Himalayas to the south, and is situated at a height of 11,400 ft (3,475 km).Ladakh is composed of Leh and Kargil districts. The region contains prominent Buddhist sites and has an ecotourism industry.

Kargil, Drass, Sankoo and Zanskar are main towns in this region. Kargil, in Ladakh, offers a range of tourist attractions including historical sites, monasteries, and natural landscapes, making it a popular destination for adventure and cultural tourism. It's known for its stunning scenery, particularly the Suru Valley and the Nun-Kun peaks. Kargil is also a hub for adventure activities like trekking and white-water rafting. Dras War Memorial: A memorial dedicated to the Kargil War, a significant event in Indian history. Phugtal Monastery: A unique cave monastery in Zanskar, known for its architectural style and scenic beauty. Suru Valley: A lush valley with green meadows and gushing streams, offering panoramic views of the Nun and Kun peaks. Nun-Kun Peaks: Majestic mountains, a popular destination for trekkers and mountaineers. Rangdum Monastery: A well-known monastery with a rich history and spiritual significance. Mulbekh Monastery: A historical monastery perched on a hilltop, offering stunning views of the valley. Drang-Drung Glacier: A large glacier, a part of the vast Himalayan range. Sankoo Village: A picturesque village in Suru Valley, a starting point for treks and a place to experience local culture.
